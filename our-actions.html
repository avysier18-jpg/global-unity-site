OUR ACTIONS

This page summarizes the concrete steps taken to call for lawful, transparent public policy. It is written for everyone: residents, advocates, journalists, and officials who want to understand what has been done—and what is still coming.

Why These Actions Exist

From Confusion to Record

Many people feel that “something is wrong” in housing, money, education, and health systems, but cannot prove it in a way officials are forced to answer. Formal actions—especially under the Administrative Procedure Act (APA)—turn that intuition into a documented record.

Using the Rules, Not Breaking Them

The filings described on this page do not bypass the law—they use it. The goal is not to attack any person, but to put lawful pressure on agencies so that policies, data, and decisions can be explained and, when necessary, changed.

Connected to the Truth Library

The Truth Library explains patterns. Our Actions are where those patterns are presented to agencies and courts with specific questions, evidence, and legal arguments. Together, they form a Mirror between lived reality and official records.

Administrative Procedure Act (APA) & Oversight Actions

The APA gives the public a way to ask formal questions about agency decisions and to challenge rules that may be arbitrary, unsupported, or inconsistent with law. Below is a high-level map of APA-related work and other formal oversight efforts.

Federal Reserve & Monetary Policy

Actions asking the Fed to explain how it balances employment, inflation, and financial stability, and how its choices interact with the documented gap between prices and wages.

Focus areas include: impact on housing markets, credit conditions, and distributional effects across wage earners vs asset holders.

View Fed-related materials →

HUD, FHFA & Housing Programs

Oversight questions directed at federal housing agencies, focusing on rental burdens, use of public funds, treatment of renters vs investors, and the practical outcomes of programs meant to ensure “affordable” housing.

Evidence includes data tables, program documents, and case-based examples.

View housing-program materials →

Treasury & Cross-Agency Coordination

Work examining how Treasury and related entities coordinate with the Fed and housing agencies, especially in times of crisis, and how those decisions affect everyday people.

View Treasury & coordination notes →

Education & Human Services

Questions directed at education and health agencies regarding how funding formulas, eligibility rules, and enforcement priorities interact with the lived realities documented in the human impact evidence.

Timeline of Major Steps

This is a simplified timeline for anyone who wants to see the sequence: when concerns were raised, when questions were asked, and when formal filings were made. Dates are placeholders that can be updated as filings are finalized.

2023 · Initial Research & Pattern Mapping

Collection of public data on inflation, wages, housing costs, and program outcomes. Early drafts of the Truth Library rooms and first internal summaries of systemic patterns.

2024 · Truth Library v1 & Community Site

Launch of the first version of the Global Unity site and the Truth Library framework, designed to make complex systems visible in calm, clear language.

2024–2025 · Formal Oversight Actions

Preparation and submission of APA and related oversight materials targeting housing, monetary policy, and program outcomes, with evidence linked in the Transparency section.

Next Steps · Ongoing Monitoring & Response

Tracking agency responses, court decisions, and further data releases, and updating the Truth Library and evidence folders to reflect what changes—and what does not.
